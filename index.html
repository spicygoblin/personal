// about

I'm a PhD candidate in Industrial-Organizational Psychology with a focus on people analytics — the practice of using behavioral science and data to understand how organizations function and how to make them better for the people inside them.

My background is unusual: I combine IO methodology (psychometrics, survey design, causal inference) with computational approaches like NLP and machine learning applied to organizational data. I've worked in people analytics at the Port Authority of NY & NJ and in HR modernization at ETS.

Outside of research I write fiction, build things on the web, and think a lot about systems — how rules interact, how small changes ripple, how worlds hold together. It turns out this is also what makes someone good at organizational modeling.
